Urgent Blood Donations Needed
All hosptials and clinics are in need of all blood types. For more information, please visit your local blood donation center or contact the Palestinian Red Crescent Society.

Before Donating (Preparation is Key)
1. Hydrate: Drink plenty of water before donating to make the process smoother.
2. Get a good night's sleep (7–9 hours) the night before.
3. Eat a healthy meal: Have a balanced, low-fat meal 2–3 hours before (e.g., lean proteins, fruits, veggies, iron-rich foods like spinach, beans, red meat, or fortified cereals). Pair with vitamin C-rich foods (citrus, berries) to help iron absorption. Avoid fatty foods like burgers, fries, or ice cream—they can affect blood processing
During the Donation
1. Relax: Stay calm and comfortable during the donation. Deep breaths can help.
2. Follow instructions: Listen to the staff and follow their guidance for a safe donation.
3.The whole process usually takes about 45–60 minutes, with the actual blood draw lasting 8–10 minutes for whole blood.
Staff will check your vitals (blood pressure, temperature, hemoglobin) and ask health questions for safety.
After Donating (Recovery Tips)
1. Rest: Take it easy for the rest of the day. Avoid heavy lifting or strenuous activities for at least 24 hours.
2. Hydrate and eat: Drink plenty of fluids and have a nutritious meal to help replenish your energy.
3. Care for the site: Keep the bandage on for a few hours (or as instructed). Avoid heavy lifting, strenuous exercise, or vigorous activity with that arm for the rest of the day. If it bleeds, apply pressure and elevate your arm. Use a cold pack for bruising.
4. Watch for side effects: Mild dizziness, lightheadedness, or fatigue is common—sit or lie down with feet elevated if it happens. Contact the center if symptoms persist or worsen.

Please take care of yourselves and each other during this critical time. Your donation can save lives. Thank you for your generosity and support!
